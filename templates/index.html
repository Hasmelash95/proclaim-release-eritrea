What Is Release Eritrea?

Release Eritrea is a global partnership of Eritrean Evangelicals standing in solidarity with the persecuted Eritrean church.

On May 22 2002, the PFDJ (the ruling party of Eritrea) ordered the closure of all churches not belonging to the Orthodox, Roman Catholic or Evangelical Lutheran denominations.

On several occasions since then the Eritrean government issued blanket denials of the existence of persecution in the country stating that 'no groups or persons are persecuted in Eritrea for their beliefs or religion' and that people were 'free to worship according to their wish'.

The unitary one-party presidential prepublic is considered by Human Rights Watch to have among the worst human rights record in the world.

What We Do

We call upon Eritreans, friends of Eritrea and members of the international community to pray and call upon the Government of Eritrea to restore freedom of worship to members of independent Protestant churches.

We fundraise and accept donations to help refugees who have manged to escape to other countries and the families of prisoners of Faith who yet remain in captivity.

We write to our Members of Parliament and speak directly in the House of Parliament to ask to put in place strategic pressure on the Eritrean government to respect the religious rights of Eritrean believers.
